I am currently a second-year PhD student in the [Department of Biostatistics](https://www.brown.edu/academics/public-health/biostats/home) at Brown University, where I am advised by Ani Eloyan. My research involves developing new statistical and machine learning methods to use technological advances to improve care for people with complex neurological conditions like Alzheimer's and Parkinson's diseases. My current project focuses on using manifold learning approaches to reach smooth estimates of the structures underlying neuroimaging data.

Prior to my time at Brown, I received my Bachelors of Arts degree in Statistics and Economics from [Amherst College](https://amherst.edu), and I spent two years with the University of Rochester's [Center for Health + Technology](https://www.urmc.rochester.edu/health-technology.aspx).

## Current Interests and Projects:

- Manifold Learning
- Topological Data Analysis
- Medical Imaging
- Neurological Diseases
